Environment:

Trees that are a combination of jungle forest and red wood forests but even bigger at times

  • outskirts are average sized red wood jungletrees
  • These trees vary in width from tiny sapling, to multiple city blocks lined up together. In height, they can grow higher than the Burj Khalifa. Sometimes they exchange height for width, in order to have a stable base and so they can branch off more.
  • Due to this truly giant size, the light doesn't seep through the leaves, which is why the people living in the forest are 1, high up in the trees to get more sunshine and 2, not that much on the floor, because the light just never reaches the floor.
  • The deeper you go, the bigger the trees get
  • These forests are known for the fact that you can get lost in them
  • Only those that know how to navigate in those forests, can be seen alive after going into one
  • At night, it gets so dark, you can't even see your hand before your eyes, so the forest is lit up with biolumenescent crystals, (will be explained later) moss, vines, fireflys and special vines with glowing fruits (!special plant exclusive to this forest!)
  • Forestfloor is covered with high grass, flowers, sticks and more foreststuff
  • Inside of the forest, there are also lakes and rivers, that connect with each other and eventually flow out of the forest. It is known to be one of the only guides for outsiders exploring the forest
  • In order to get out of the forest and navigate in it, you need a guide, that shows you where to go
  • There are a few spaces where there aren't trees, but those are very rare and hard to find
  • These clearences are literal lights in the dark from the floor
  • The leaves of the rwj-trees are a saturating green and when the sun shines on them, you can see the delicate veins of the leaves
  • As soon as the height of the tree reaches at least a tenth of its' full height, it starts branching off. There are some exceptions where they start way earlier, but those trees tend to lean onto other trees
  • The biolumenescent crystals are either put into lanterns and then powered with a little fire, or they grow directly on the moss, or stonefloor, resulting in a lightly lit up forest.

Living spaces

In, outside and around the trees

  • Trees are sometimes carved out, so you can live in them, but in a way the tree isn't hurt(only those that know the trees can do that)
  • Only trees that are bigger are carved out, so they can handle losing their transportationchannels
  • The carving is made by experienced carpenters with axes
  • The carved out trees are normally the middle and the center of attention of the (quite literal) treehouse
  • The rest of the treehouse is built around the main branch, with connection to the carved out space of the tree
  • The treehouse is built around the main branch in a hexagon, if it's a bigger branch, then a rounder shape can be used
  • The treehouses may seem small from the outside, but they are quite spacey from the inside (the wonders a carpenter can do)
  • The treehouses also have balconies on the outside with railings, in order to avoid anyone just falling off the treehouse and then splashing on the ground deep below into a puddle of blood and bones
  • The treehouses also have slanted roofs with rounded shingles.Tose roofs are higher at the tree and lower at the outside of the house. They also hang over the outside wall a bit.
  • Sometimes, a carved out tree has no house around it, but instead are little storing units for the treehouses around it.
  • There are also bigger versions of these storing units, that act as storing units for community food or little collections of treehouses that are more isolated than the other treehouses.
  • The carved out trees often have balconies, from which you can step directly from inside the tree, onto the balcony.
  • The community house is used for meetings, occasionally announcements, storing, greetings and much more, a tree bigger in width than a city block and it is one of the highest trees in the forest. With that, it is also almost in the middle of the forest and basically a capital for some people.

The people living in these forests

Rankings and duties

  • The people living here escaped from a corrupt government into the forest, in order to live by their own laws and ideals.
  • They then created their own secret society with different sections, a council and a sole leader.
  • These different sections are marked by colour, signalising the tasks or workforce the person belongs to.
  • The people wear a coloured band around their upper arm and depending on what colour it is, you can find out what workforce they belong to
  • Blue is for the farmers, the ones that grow and harvest plants and that provide almost all of the food for the people.
  • Yellow is for the animalcaretakers, the ones that take care of and raise almost all of the animals. They usually live in and around clearings, in order to get some free space for their animals.
  • Black is for the miners, due to this society having a major monopoly on precious and rare metals.This is one of the reasons their allies are very fond of their place, after a bit of consideration (why the consideration you ask? Well... We'll get into that later!).
  • Purple is for the magic users, witches, enchanters, whatever you want to call them. They are a fairly recent addition, since magic is pretty nem to these people. They cast spells for protection, better harvests, health and much more, making them a now major part of this society.
  • White is for newcomers, that are still deciding what section they want to join.
  • Orange is for the watchers, the ones that stake out and locate people that want to join their secret society. Due to this, they live in the still corrupt society. That way they can reach those, that are willing to join their secret place.
  • Green is for the people that retrieve other refugees and explain to them how life in their, now new, home works. They explain the colors and their meaning, show them around, teach them how to survive in the forest and introduce them to every person they meet, during the teaching.
